About AcadTrack

Built for Filipino students,
free and private

AcadTrack helps you monitor your college progress — subjects, grades, prerequisites, and graduation timeline — all without needing an account.

What AcadTrack does

📊 GWA computation

Supports the Philippine 1.0-highest, 5.0-highest, and Benilde 4.0-highest grading systems. GWA updates live with Latin honors (Cum laude, Magna, Summa).

🔗 Prerequisite tracking

Link subjects together. Subjects with unmet prerequisites are flagged automatically so you know what to take first.

📈 Progress tracking

See your degree completion percentage based on earned units vs. total curriculum units — broken down by year and semester.

🗓 Graduation estimate

Supports semestral, trimestral, and quadrimestral calendars. AcadTrack estimates your finish date based on remaining terms.

🔮 What-if Simulator New

Assign hypothetical grades to remaining subjects and see your projected GWA update in real time. Great for academic planning.

⚠ Irregular student detection New

Automatically flags your account as Irregular if your ongoing subjects span more than one year level.

★ Credited subjects New

Mark transferred or cross-enrolled subjects as Credited. They count toward your earned units but are excluded from GWA computation.

📚 Curriculum templates

Load a pre-built curriculum to instantly populate all your subjects. Adamson BSBA Marketing Management (2022) is available now, with more coming.

🏫 50 schools supported New

Choose your school from a searchable dropdown of 50 Philippine universities across NCR, Luzon, Visayas, and Mindanao. Auto-detects grading system and calendar type.

🔒 No account needed

Everything is stored in your browser's local storage. Nothing is sent to any server. Your academic data is yours alone.

What's new

🏫
School dropdown with 50 Philippine universities New

The registration now shows a searchable list of 50 top universities grouped by region (NCR, Luzon, Visayas, Mindanao). Selecting your school auto-suggests the right grading system and calendar type. Can't find yours? Choose "Other" and type it in.

📘
Benilde / CSB grading system New

GPA-style 4.0 scale used by De La Salle – College of Saint Benilde. 4.0 = Excellent, 1.0 = minimum passing (D), 0.0 = Failed. Latin honors adjusted for the Benilde scale (Summa ≥ 3.80, Magna ≥ 3.50, Cum Laude ≥ 3.20). CSB is auto-detected when selected from the school dropdown.

📅
Trimestral & Quadrimestral calendar support New

Choose your calendar system during setup: Semestral (2 sems + summer), Trimestral (3 terms/year), or Quadrimestral (4 terms/year). Semester labels, exam period names, graduation estimates, and calendar highlights all update to match. DLSU is auto-detected as trimestral.

🔮
What-if GWA Simulator New

Found on the Dashboard next to "Add subject." Assign hypothetical grades to your remaining or failed subjects and watch your projected GWA update instantly. Nothing is saved — it's purely for planning. Supports all three grading systems.

⚠
Irregular student detection New

If you have ongoing subjects across more than one year level, the Dashboard automatically shows an ⚠ Irregular badge on your GWA card. No manual tagging needed.

★
Credited subject status New

A new Credited status (teal badge) for transferred, cross-enrolled, or advanced-credited subjects. Credited subjects count toward your earned units and satisfy prerequisites, but are excluded from GWA computation — matching how most Philippine universities handle them.

🗓
4th Quarter exam period on Schedule & Calendar New

Quadrimestral students now get a 4th Quarter exam period (teal color) on the Schedule page and calendar highlights. All exam period labels update dynamically based on your calendar type.

Your data & sync

💾

Where is my data saved?

AcadTrack saves your data in your browser's localStorage — a built-in storage area in every modern browser. It stays on your device and is never uploaded anywhere.

📵

Does it sync between my phone and PC?

No — not automatically. localStorage is specific to the browser and device you're using. If you add subjects on your phone, those changes will not appear on your PC's browser, and vice versa.

This also means: if you clear your browser data or use a different browser on the same device, your tracker data may be lost. Always export regularly.

⚡ How to use AcadTrack across multiple devices

Use the Export and Import feature (Settings → Data & backup) to move your data between devices manually.

1
On your current device: open Settings ⚙ on the dashboard and click Export to file. A .json file will download.
2
Transfer the file to your other device — via Google Drive, email, Messenger, USB, or any method you prefer.
3
On the new device: go to AcadTrack in your browser, open Settings ⚙, and click Import from file. Select the .json file.
4
Your tracker is now synced. Repeat whenever you make major updates on one device.

Tip: Pick one primary device (e.g. your PC) for updating grades, and use Export/Import whenever you want to view it on your phone. Automatic cloud sync may be added in a future version.

About the project

AcadTrack was built to help Filipino college students stay on top of their academic progress without needing to maintain messy spreadsheets or rely on school portals that can be hard to navigate.

It's designed to work for any Philippine university — whether you're in UP, Ateneo, La Salle, Adamson, UST, Benilde, or any other school — with support for all major grading systems and calendar types used across the country.

Your privacy matters. AcadTrack does not track your usage, collect analytics, show ads, or send your data anywhere. Everything lives in your browser.
